Chocolate cookies

The best recipe for double chocolate chip cookies must obviously include extra doses of chocolate. These soft-baked thick and chunky cookies are as indulgent as they look: rich and fudge-like with chewy centers, slightly crisp edges, and oodles of melty chocolate chips in each glorious bite.

Ingrediant

  • Wet ingredients: Butter, white & brown sugars, egg, vanilla extract, and milk
  • Dry ingredients: Flour, unsweetened natural cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt

Directions

  1. Easy to make with a basic dough
  2. Soft-baked and chewy with fudge chocolate centers
  3. Recipe is easily doubled
  4. Cookie dough balls freeze well
  5. You can use the cookies to make chocolate

Tips

The dough definitely comes together easily, but it’s sticky and tacky as a result of the cocoa powder mixing with liquid. It’s even stickier than the chocolate layer of pinwheel cookies. Therefore, the dough requires chilling before you can shape and bake. I recommend at least 3 hours in the refrigerator, but you can also refrigerate it overnight
